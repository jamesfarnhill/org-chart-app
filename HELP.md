# Account Power Map — User Guide

A practical guide to building and using your account map. The same content is available
in‑app any time via the **? Help** button in the top bar.

> This is not an HR org chart. It is a **map of the buying committee** — built to answer:
> who decides, who backs us, who blocks us, and where do we have no coverage?

---

## 1. The big picture

You build a picture of an account by adding **contacts**, saying how senior/influential they
are, how they feel about you (**disposition**), what **role** they play in the buying decision,
and how they **connect** to each other. The tool then shows you that picture three ways and tells
you where your risks are.

Everything saves automatically in your browser. There is nothing to set up.

---

## 2. The three views (top-bar tabs)

| View | What it shows | Use it to… |
|------|----------------|------------|
| **Org map** | The reporting tree. Cards are coloured by disposition, badged with buying roles, and show influence as dots. | Build and read the structure. |
| **Power map** | Everyone plotted by **influence** (vertical) × **disposition** (horizontal). | See your strategy at a glance: top‑right = mobilise, top‑left = convert/neutralise, bottom = lower priority. |
| **Coverage** | An automatic deal‑risk **score** and a prioritised **punch‑list** of gaps. | Decide what to do next. |

---

## 3. Building it out

### Add contacts
- **Top‑right `＋ Contact`** adds a top‑level person.
- **Hover a card** and use the **`＋` on top** (add a manager above) or the **`＋` on the bottom**
  (add a direct report).

### Edit a contact
- **Click any card** to open the **detail panel** on the right.
- Name and job title can be edited **inline on the card** or in the panel.

In the detail panel you can set, with one click each:
- **Disposition** — Champion, Coach, Neutral, Detractor.
- **Influence (1–5)** — real power in this account, *not* their title.
- **Buying roles** — Economic Buyer, Deal Champion, Technical Champion, Influencer, End User,
  Procurement, Legal, Security, Blocker. A person can hold several.
- **Cursor owner** and **strength** (None → Strong) — who at Cursor owns the relationship.
- **LinkedIn URL**, **next step**, **what they care about**, and **notes**.

### Delete a contact
- The **×** on the card, the **Delete** key while it’s selected, or **Delete contact** in the panel.
- Their direct reports are re‑attached to their manager so the tree stays intact.

---

## 4. Layout and changing reporting

**The board arranges itself automatically** into a tidy tree — parents stay centred over their
reports, spacing stays even, and cards never overlap. Add or remove anyone and the whole
hierarchy reflows to make room.

- **Re‑parent by drag:** drag a card **onto another card** (it highlights green) to make it — and
  its whole subtree — that card’s **report**. Drop on empty space and it snaps back.
- **Or use the “Reports to” picker** in the detail panel (including **Top level**), or the **＋**
  buttons on a card (top = manager, bottom = report).
- **Fit** (canvas toolbar) zooms to show the whole map.

---

## 5. Relationships (the coloured links) — full walkthrough

Links capture the politics the reporting tree can’t:

| Link type | Meaning | Style |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| **Influences** | A sways B’s opinion (directional) | solid, arrow |
| **Dotted‑line report** | Matrixed / functional reporting | dashed, arrow |
| **Allied with** | Coalition / mutual support | solid |
| **Tension with** | Friction / rivalry | dashed |

### Create a link
1. **Click the contact** you want to link *from* (e.g. Tom Bridger).
2. In the panel, scroll to **Relationships**.
3. In **“Add a link from … to:”** choose the other **contact** and the **link type**.
4. Click **`＋ Add link`**.

### Edit a link
- Each link in the list has its own **type** dropdown, a **strength** control (1–3), and a
  **note** field — change them in place.

### Delete a link
- Click the **×** next to the link in the list.

### Show / hide and filter links on the map
- The **Links** button in the top bar shows or hides all links on the Org map.
- In the **legend (bottom‑left)**, under **“Links · click to filter”**, click a link type to show
  **only** that type. Click several to **combine** them (e.g. click **Tension with** to see only
  tension lines; also click **Influences** to see both). Click again to clear.

---

## 6. Finding and focusing

- **Filter by disposition:** click the colours in the legend (bottom‑left). Matches stay; the rest
  dim so you keep context.
- **Power blindspots:** a toggle that spotlights high‑influence people you have no relationship with.
- **Search / jump:** type in the **filter box** in the top bar, or press **⌘K / Ctrl‑K** to open
  the command palette and jump straight to a person (it recenters the map on them).

---

## 7. Reading the Coverage view

The score weights your **key people** (high influence or with a buying role) by how strong a
relationship you have. The punch‑list flags the things that lose deals:

- High‑influence people you have **no relationship** with (power blindspots).
- Powerful **detractors / blockers**.
- **No Economic Buyer** or **no Champion** identified.
- Key relationships with **no Cursor owner**.
- **Single‑threaded risk** — one champion and fewer than three other engaged contacts.

Click any flagged name to jump to them.

---

## 8. Saving, sharing and shortcuts

- **Autosave:** everything persists locally in your browser.
- **Export / Import:** JSON backups (top bar). Import adds the account alongside your others.
- **Print:** produces a clean PDF of the current view.
- **Multiple accounts:** use the **`＋`** next to the account name to add one; switch with the
  dropdown that appears once you have more than one.
- **Shortcuts:** Undo `⌘Z` / Redo `⇧⌘Z`, jump `⌘K`, **Delete** removes the selected contact,
  **Esc** deselects.

---

## 9. A suggested first‑time workflow

1. Rename the account (top‑left).
2. Add the people you know, roughly in reporting order (use the card `＋` buttons).
3. For each, set **disposition**, **influence**, and any **buying roles**.
4. Add **relationships** for the influence and tension you know about.
5. Open **Coverage** and work the list top‑down.
6. Switch to the **Power map** before a deal review — it tells the story in one screen.
